I am an undergraduate at UC San Diego (UCSD) studying marine biology and computer science.

I study evolution and bioluminescence in marine invertebrates at Scripps Institution of Oceanography (SIO) with Prof. Greg Rouse and Dr. Dimitri Deheyn, and develop phylogenetics tools at the San Diego Supercomputer Center (SDSC). Last summer I worked with Prof. Steve Haddock at the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute (MBARI) on characterizing protist photoproteins.

Current Projects

    Rouse Lab @ SIO

    A new species of deep-sea, wood-eating asteroid of the genus Xyloplax was found from the Juan de Fuca Ridge hydrothermal vent system, off the coast of Oregon, US.

    Visit my Marine Invertebrates Lab bio.

  2. Biochemistry of new octocoral bioluminescence
    Deheyn Lab @ SIO

    Assays and purification techniques are used to describe the properties of and reaction(s) responsible for light emission from a new species of octocoral with a seemingly novel bioluminescence system.
